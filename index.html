The Problem

Three structural failures

The ACA mandates zero-cost preventive care, but three legal and billing loopholes mean patients still pay — often without warning, and at precisely the moments they most need care.

Failure 01
The Age Gap
USPSTF lowered the colorectal cancer screening age from 50 to 45 in 2021, but adults aged 20–44 remain entirely excluded from zero-cost-share protection — even those with documented hereditary risk. This is a policy gap, not a billing error.
CRC is now the #1 cancer killer under 50
Failure 02
The Dual Billing Trap
When a patient mentions a symptom during a preventive visit, insurers may split the visit into two charges — applying a separate Evaluation & Management fee directly to the patient's deductible. Patients arrive expecting $0 and leave with an unexpected bill. No advance notice is required under Florida law.
47% delayed care due to unexpected costs
Failure 03
The Diagnostic Reclassification Trap
When a screening colonoscopy finds a polyp, it is reclassified as "diagnostic" — stripping it of zero-cost-share status. Ancillary charges for bowel prep, anesthesia, and pathology then fall to the patient. The 2023 CAA fixed the colonoscopy itself for most plans, but grandfathered plans and ancillary costs remain unresolved.
53% still pay out-of-pocket for colonoscopy bowel prep

Proposed Solutions

Four targeted reforms

Each reform targets a specific structural failure with a concrete policy mechanism, grounded in existing precedent or legislation.

01
Expedited USPSTF At-Risk Interim Grade B Pathway
Establish a fast-track mechanism for USPSTF to issue Interim Grade B recommendations extending zero-cost-share CRC screening to high-risk adults under 45 — closing the age gap without waiting for full guideline revision cycles.
→ Targets the Age Gap
02
Extend Florida SB 158 to CRC Ancillary Costs
Florida SB 158 (effective Jan 2026) eliminated cost-sharing for supplemental breast imaging follow-up. Apply the same model to colonoscopy ancillary costs — bowel prep, anesthesia, and pathology — for state-regulated plans.
→ Targets the Reclassification Trap
03
Bronze Plan CRC Affordability Mandate
Require ACA marketplace Bronze plans to cover at minimum one CRC screening modality at zero cost-share annually, regardless of deductible status — eliminating cost as a barrier for the lowest-income insured Floridians.
→ Targets Bronze Plan Exposure
04
FIT Testing at Annual Wellness Visits
Mandate FIT stool testing (~$24/test) as a standard component of ACA annual wellness visits. COVID-era bulk procurement modeling suggests unit costs could fall below $5, making early CRC detection affordable at population scale.
→ Targets Access & Cost
